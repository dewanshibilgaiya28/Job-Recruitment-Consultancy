Resume Tips

Short, focused guidance to make your resume get noticed.

1) Keep it concise

  • Prefer 1 page for early-career, 1–2 pages otherwise.
  • Use short bullet points (achievement → metric).

2) Show results, not responsibilities

  • Write achievements using metrics ("+30%", "reduced X by Y").
  • Start bullets with strong verbs ("Led", "Improved", "Delivered").

3) Match the job

  • Read the job description and include the most relevant keywords.
  • Put the most relevant experience near the top of each section.

4) Format & deliver

  • Use a clean font, consistent spacing and bullet style.
  • Export and upload as PDF when asked (preserves layout).
  • Name files clearly: Firstname_Lastname_Resume.pdf.

Quick checklist

  1. No typos — run a spell check.
  2. Contact info is current.
  3. Top 3 achievements are obvious within the first screen.
